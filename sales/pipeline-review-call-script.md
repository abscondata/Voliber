# Pipeline Review Call Script

## Opening

Thanks for taking the call. I will keep this simple.

Voliber helps B2B service firms keep a weekly queue for revenue that is already in motion: unpaid invoices, old proposals, failed payments, and dormant leads. The goal is to see what exists, what can be followed up on safely, and what needs approval before anyone touches it.

The main question is:

Do you have unpaid invoices, old proposals, or dormant leads that no one consistently owns?

## Problem Framing

In a lot of service businesses, the issue is not that no one cares. It is that follow-up sits between roles.

Sales thinks finance has it. Finance thinks account managers have the relationship. Account managers do not want to create tension. So the item sits there until cash gets tight or the client asks about it.

The Pipeline Review is meant to find those items, sort them, and decide what should happen next.

## Diagnostic Questions

- Where does follow-up usually get dropped today?
- Which is more common for you: unpaid invoices, old proposals, failed payments, or dormant leads?
- Who owns follow-up once an invoice or proposal is sent?
- How often does someone review stale items today?
- Are there customers you would rather handle personally?
- What would make follow-up feel useful instead of annoying?
- What dollar amount makes an item worth working?
- What usually blocks movement: lack of time, unclear owner, sensitive relationship, or bad data?

## Data And Source Questions

- Where do invoices live?
- Where do proposals or estimates live?
- Where do leads and opportunities live?
- Do you use QuickBooks, HubSpot, Stripe, a proposal tool, spreadsheets, or something else?
- Can you export open invoices, stale proposals, dormant leads, and failed payments?
- Do those records show last touch date or current status?
- Are notes and owner fields reliable?
- Would you prefer to export files manually or provide read-only access?

## Approval And Risk Questions

- What types of follow-up are already approved?
- What items should never be touched without approval?
- Are any accounts sensitive, strategic, or owner-managed?
- What tone should follow-up use?
- What channels are allowed?
- What should happen if a customer disputes the amount or service?
- Who approves discounts, credits, payment plans, or exceptions?
- What conditions should stop follow-up immediately?

## Close For Next Step

The next step is a focused Pipeline Review.

You send or export the source data we agree on. Voliber maps the open revenue objects, sorts them into an A/B/C queue, flags approval-required items, and gives you a 45-day follow-up plan. From there, you can decide whether to work the queue internally or have Voliber run the approved follow-up cadence.

If that makes sense, the next step is to confirm the source systems, the export method, and the approval rules.
