# Objection Handling

## We Already Follow Up Internally.

That can still be a fit. Voliber is not replacing your team. The question is whether every unpaid invoice, old proposal, failed payment, and dormant lead has a weekly owner, next action, and touch log. If that is already true, you may not need us.

## We Do Not Want To Annoy Clients.

That is the right concern. Voliber only works from approved tone, channels, timing, and stop conditions. Sensitive accounts and disputed items are routed back before follow-up.

## Is This Collections?

No. Voliber is not a collections agency and does not use legal pressure or debt collection tactics. The work is operational follow-up on revenue objects the client has approved for contact.

## We Do Not Have A Lot Of Overdue Invoices.

That may be fine. The review also looks at old proposals, dormant leads, and failed payments. If there is not enough open revenue to justify the work, we should say that early.

## We Use QuickBooks, HubSpot, Etc. Already.

Good. Voliber does not replace those systems. We use exports or read-only views to build a weekly queue, prioritize items, log touches, and report movement.

## What Exactly Do You Need Access To?

Usually exports are enough to start: open invoices, stale proposals or estimates, dormant leads, failed payments, and recent touch history if available. Read-only access can help, but it is not required for the first review.

## How Do You Charge?

The first step is a paid Pipeline Review. After that, ongoing queue work can be scoped based on volume, touch rules, and reporting needs. We should price it only after seeing the size and complexity of the queue.

## What If The Customer Disputes The Invoice?

Follow-up stops and the item is routed back for review. Voliber does not handle disputed items without approval.

## Can You Guarantee Recovered Revenue?

No. Voliber is responsible for disciplined review, prioritization, approved follow-up, logging, and reporting. We cannot guarantee that a customer will pay, reply, or move forward.

## Why Not Just Hire A VA?

A VA may be useful for task volume. Voliber is narrower: classify open revenue, prioritize the queue, follow approved touch rules, route exceptions, and report movement. The value is control and consistency around revenue follow-up, not general admin capacity.
