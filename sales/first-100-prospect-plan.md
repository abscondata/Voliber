# First 100 Prospect Plan

## Target Verticals

Start with B2B service firms and agencies where invoices, proposals, retainers, and account relationships are normal.

Priority verticals:

- Marketing agencies
- Web design and development firms
- IT service providers
- Managed service providers
- Business consultants
- Operations consultants
- Small professional service firms

Defer for now:

- Consumer debt businesses
- Medical billing or collections
- Legal collections
- Ecommerce chargeback-heavy businesses
- Large enterprises
- Very small solo operators with low invoice value

## Search Criteria

Use practical filters that suggest an owner-led firm with enough revenue motion to justify a Pipeline Review.

- B2B service business
- 5 to 75 employees
- Clear service offering
- Project, retainer, consulting, or support model
- Founder, owner, operator, finance, or client services buyer visible
- Uses invoices, proposals, CRM, or payment tools
- Located in English-speaking target markets first
- No obvious regulated collections, medical billing, or legal pressure model

## Buyer Titles

Primary:

- Founder
- Owner
- Managing Partner
- CEO
- COO

Secondary:

- Operations Manager
- Finance Manager
- Controller
- Head of Client Services
- Account Director

## Data Sources

Manual sources:

- Company websites
- LinkedIn company pages
- LinkedIn people search
- Google Maps and local business search
- Agency directories
- Clutch, DesignRush, GoodFirms, and similar directories
- Industry association directories
- Public case study or portfolio pages

Tools to use later:

- Apollo
- Instantly
- Clay
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- BuiltWith or similar site research tools
- Email verification tools

## Fields To Collect

Use [prospect-list-schema.csv](prospect-list-schema.csv) as the source of truth.

Required before outreach:

- Company name
- Website
- Industry
- City
- State
- Employee count or estimate
- Buyer name
- Buyer title
- Buyer email or reliable contact path
- Source
- Open revenue signal
- Reason for fit
- Priority
- Touch status

Optional but useful:

- Estimated revenue range
- LinkedIn URL
- Notes on tools used
- Notes on proposal, invoice, or retainer signals

## Quality Rules

- Do not fabricate company names, buyers, emails, signals, or revenue ranges.
- Do not add a prospect without a real source.
- Do not claim the company has overdue invoices unless there is a public or directly confirmed signal.
- Use "likely" only when the business model supports the inference.
- Prefer named buyers over generic inboxes.
- Prioritize owner/operator visibility over company size.
- Exclude prospects with obvious collection, dispute, legal, or medical billing complexity.
- Keep notes factual and short.

## Daily Target

- Research 20 companies per day.
- Keep 12 to 15 if they meet A-fit or strong B-fit criteria.
- Find at least one reachable buyer for each kept company.
- Send only to prospects with a clear reason for fit.
- Review and clean the list at the end of each day.

## First-Week Goal

- Build 100 researched prospects.
- Keep at least 60 A-fit or strong B-fit prospects.
- Identify the most common vertical and buyer-title pattern.
- Send the first controlled outbound batch only after the list has been reviewed for fit, source quality, and no fabricated data.
- Track replies, objections, and call bookings in the prospect list.
