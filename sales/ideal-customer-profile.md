# Ideal Customer Profile

## Primary Vertical

B2B service firms and agencies.

Examples:

- Marketing agencies
- Web design firms
- IT service providers
- Consulting firms
- Small professional service firms

## Why This Vertical Is First

B2B service firms usually sell through proposals, projects, retainers, and invoice terms. That means revenue can stall in several places: unpaid invoices, old proposals, missed follow-up, failed payments, and dormant opportunities. These firms often have enough deal value for follow-up discipline to matter, but not enough internal operating capacity to keep every item owned every week.

This market is also easier to qualify without making collection-style claims. The sales conversation can stay focused on process ownership, approved follow-up, documentation, and movement of existing revenue.

## Best-Fit Traits

- Sells B2B services, retainers, projects, or recurring support
- Uses invoices, estimates, proposals, or CRM opportunities
- Has a founder, owner, operator, or finance lead close to revenue
- Has deal sizes large enough that a few recovered or revived items matter
- Has a visible gap between sales, delivery, finance, and follow-up ownership
- Wants calm, documented follow-up without legal pressure
- Can export basic invoice, proposal, lead, or failed-payment data
- Has enough customer relationship sensitivity to value approval rules

## Disqualifiers

- Mainly consumer debt or individual debtor collection
- Medical billing or regulated collections
- Highly disputed accounts as the normal case
- Very low invoice values where follow-up effort has poor economics
- Enterprise buying process with no reachable owner or operator
- Needs legal action, credit reporting, skip tracing, or debt collection
- Cannot provide basic source data or approval rules
- Wants aggressive pressure tactics

## Buyer Titles

- Founder
- Owner
- Managing Partner
- CEO
- COO
- Operations Manager
- Finance Manager
- Controller
- Head of Client Services
- Account Director

## Likely Pain Points

- Invoices are sent, but follow-up is inconsistent
- Proposals go quiet after the first send
- Leads and past conversations sit in the CRM without an owner
- Payment failures are visible, but no one works them weekly
- Account managers avoid awkward money conversations
- Owners only notice stale revenue when cash gets tight
- Tools show the data, but no one runs the queue
- Follow-up happens reactively instead of on a documented cadence

## What Not To Target Yet

- Consumer collections
- Medical, dental, or insurance receivables
- Legal collections or debt-buying businesses
- Property management arrears
- Large enterprises with formal credit departments
- Ecommerce chargebacks or fraud-heavy payment disputes
- Businesses that need legal, accounting, or bookkeeping cleanup before follow-up
- Companies that want guaranteed recovery or aggressive collections language
